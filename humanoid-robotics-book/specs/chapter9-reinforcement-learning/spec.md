# Feature Specification: Chapter 9 - Introduction to Humanoid Robots: History and Design

**Feature Branch**: `book-ch9-humanoid-intro`  
**Created**: 2025-12-07  
**Status**: Draft  
**Input**: User request to complete book content for Chapter 9.

## User Scenarios & Testing

### User Story 1 - Understand the Rationale for Humanoid Robots (Priority: P1)

The reader wants to understand the motivations and advantages behind designing robots in a humanoid form, especially in human-centric environments.

**Why this priority**: Establishes the unique value proposition and design philosophy of humanoid robots.

**Independent Test**: The reader can explain key reasons for building humanoid robots (e.g., adaptability to human environments, intuitive interaction).

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1.  **Given** a reader new to humanoid robotics, **When** they read Section 9.1, **Then** they can articulate why a human-like form is beneficial for certain robot applications.
2.  **Given** a reader comparing robot designs, **When** they read Section 9.1, **Then** they can identify the advantages of humanoids in spaces designed for humans.

### User Story 2 - Learn the History of Famous Humanoid Robots (Priority: P2)

The reader wants a brief historical overview of significant humanoid robots and their contributions to the field.

**Why this priority**: Provides context and highlights key milestones in humanoid robot development.

**Independent Test**: The reader can identify and briefly describe famous humanoid robots like ASIMO and Atlas.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1.  **Given** a reader interested in robotics history, **When** they read Section 9.2, **Then** they can recall major advancements through examples of specific humanoid robots.
2.  **Given** a reader exploring the evolution of humanoids, **When** they read Section 9.2, **Then** they can understand the progression of capabilities in this field.

### User Story 3 - Recognize Key Design Challenges (Priority: P1)

The reader wants to understand the primary engineering and design challenges inherent in creating functional humanoid robots.

**Why this priority**: Highlights the complexity and ongoing research areas in humanoid development.

**Independent Test**: The reader can identify key challenges such as power management, weight distribution, and overall system complexity.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1.  **Given** a reader considering the practicality of humanoids, **When** they read Section 9.3, **Then** they can understand the major technical hurdles faced by engineers.
2.  **Given** a reader evaluating humanoid robot performance, **When** they read Section 9.3, **Then** they can explain how factors like power consumption and weight impact design choices.

## Requirements

### Functional Requirements

-   **FR-001**: Chapter MUST explain the rationale and advantages of building humanoid robots.
-   **FR-002**: Chapter MUST provide a brief history of prominent humanoid robots.
-   **FR-003**: Chapter MUST outline the key design challenges associated with humanoid robotics.
-   **FR-004**: Chapter MUST use clear and accessible language.
-   **FR-005**: Chapter MUST maintain consistency with the book's overall structure and tone.

### Key Entities

-   **Humanoid Robot**: A robot with its overall shape based on that of a human body.
-   **ASIMO**: Advanced Step in Innovative Mobility, a series of humanoid robots created by Honda.
-   **Atlas**: A highly mobile humanoid robot developed by Boston Dynamics.
-   **Power Management**: The design and control of energy consumption and supply in a robot.
-   **Weight Distribution**: The careful balance of mass within a robot for stability and movement efficiency.
-   **Complexity**: The intricate nature of designing, building, and controlling systems with many degrees of freedom and interacting components.

## Success Criteria

-   **SC-002**: Readers can articulate the primary reasons and challenges for developing humanoid robots.
-   **SC-003**: Readers have a basic historical context for humanoid robotics.
-   **SC-004**: The chapter effectively introduces the unique aspects of humanoid robot design.
