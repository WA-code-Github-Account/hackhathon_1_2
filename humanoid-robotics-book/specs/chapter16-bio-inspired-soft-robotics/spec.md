## User Scenarios & Testing

### User Story 1 - Understand Biomimicry in Robotics (Priority: P1)

The reader wants to grasp how principles and designs from biology are inspiring new approaches in robotics.

**Why this priority**: Highlights a major interdisciplinary trend in advanced robotics development.

**Independent Test**: The reader can explain the concept of biomimicry and provide examples of how nature inspires robot design.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1.  **Given** a reader interested in innovative robot design, **When** they read Section 16.1, **Then** they can identify how biological systems inform robotic solutions.
2.  **Given** a reader considering future robot capabilities, **When** they read Section 16.1, **Then** they can understand the advantages of mimicking natural structures and functions.

### User Story 2 - Learn about Soft Robotics: Principles and Materials (Priority: P1)

The reader wants to understand the core principles, unique materials, and construction methods of soft robots.

**Why this priority**: Introduces a paradigm shift from rigid to compliant robot structures, with implications for safety and versatility.

**Independent Test**: The reader can describe what soft robotics is and the types of materials and actuation methods used.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1.  **Given** a reader exploring new robot technologies, **When** they read Section 16.2, **Then** they can differentiate soft robots from traditional rigid robots.
2.  **Given** a reader interested in robot-human interaction, **When** they read Section 16.2, **Then** they can understand how soft robots offer inherent safety advantages.

### User Story 3 - Explore Applications of Bio-inspired and Soft Robots (Priority: P2)

The reader wants to learn about the various practical applications where bio-inspired and soft robots offer unique advantages.

**Why this priority**: Showcases the value proposition of these new robotic approaches in specific domains.

**Independent Test**: The reader can identify specific application areas where bio-inspired and soft robots excel (e.g., medicine, exploration).

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1.  **Given** a reader seeking new robot solutions, **When** they read Section 16.3, **Then** they can discover how these robots address challenges rigid robots cannot.
2.  **Given** a reader evaluating future robot markets, **When** they read Section 16.3, **Then** they can identify the sectors where soft and bio-inspired robots are likely to thrive.

## Requirements

### Functional Requirements

-   **FR-001**: Chapter MUST introduce the concept of biomimicry and its role in robotics.
-   **FR-002**: Chapter MUST explain the principles, materials, and actuation of soft robotics.
-   **FR-003**: Chapter MUST discuss various applications for bio-inspired and soft robots.
-   **FR-004**: Chapter MUST use clear and accessible language.
-   **FR-005**: Chapter MUST maintain consistency with the book's overall structure and tone.

### Key Entities

-   **Biomimicry**: The imitation of the models, systems, and elements of nature for the purpose of solving complex human problems.
-   **Soft Robotics**: A subfield of robotics that deals with constructing robots from highly compliant materials, similar to those found in living organisms.
-   **Compliant Materials**: Materials that are flexible and deformable, often used in soft robotics.
-   **Applications**: The practical uses or purposes to which a technology is put.
