# Chapter 9: Introduction to Humanoid Robots: History and Design

This chapter introduces the fascinating world of humanoid robots, exploring the rationale behind their human-like form, their historical development, and the significant engineering challenges involved in their creation.

## 9.1 Why Build Humanoid Robots? The Human-Centric World

This section discusses the compelling reasons for developing robots in a humanoid form. It will emphasize their adaptability to environments designed for humans, the potential for intuitive human-robot interaction, and their versatility in performing tasks tailored for human capabilities.

## 9.2 A Brief History of Famous Humanoid Robots (e.g., ASIMO, Atlas)

We will take a journey through the history of humanoid robotics, highlighting key milestones and iconic robots. This section will feature prominent examples like Honda's ASIMO and Boston Dynamics' Atlas, showcasing their contributions to the field and the technological advancements they represent.

## 9.3 Key Design Challenges: Power, Weight, and Complexity

This section delves into the inherent difficulties in designing and building functional humanoid robots. We will cover critical challenges such as managing power consumption, optimizing weight distribution for balance and agility, and handling the immense mechanical and computational complexity of multi-limbed, highly articulate systems.
