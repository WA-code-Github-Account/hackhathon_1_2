# Dialogue Generator Skill

## Purpose
Creates natural, engaging, and character-appropriate dialogues that advance the story and reveal character personalities.

## Usage
Use this skill when writing conversations between characters, creating realistic speech patterns, and developing character voices.

## Instructions
When generating dialogue:
1. Consider each character's personality, background, and emotional state
2. Use distinct speaking styles for different characters
3. Include subtext and implied meanings
4. Add dialogue tags and action beats appropriately
5. Ensure dialogue serves the story (reveals character, advances plot, or builds tension)
6. Vary sentence length and rhythm for natural flow
7. Include interruptions, pauses, and realistic speech patterns

## Dialogue Elements

### Character Voice Guidelines
- **Protagonist**: Clear, relatable, matches their age and background
- **Antagonist**: Distinctive, potentially threatening or manipulative
- **Supporting Characters**: Unique traits, memorable speech patterns
- **Background Characters**: Brief, functional, realistic

### Emotional Tones
- Anger: Short sentences, sharp words, interruptions
- Fear: Fragmented speech, trailing off, questions
- Joy: Upbeat language, exclamations, longer sentences
- Sadness: Quiet tone, simple words, pauses
- Tension: Clipped responses, loaded silences, subtext

## Dialogue Types

### **Conflict Dialogue**
- Opposing viewpoints
- Rising tension
- Power dynamics
- Unresolved issues

### **Exposition Dialogue**
- Revealing information naturally
- Avoiding info-dumps
- Using questions and answers
- Maintaining engagement

### **Relationship Dialogue**
- Building connections
- Showing chemistry or tension
- Revealing history
- Developing bonds

### **Action Dialogue**
- Quick, punchy exchanges
- Minimal tags
- Integrated with action
- High energy

## Best Practices
- Read dialogue aloud to test naturalness
- Each character should have a distinct voice
- Use contractions for casual speech
- Avoid excessive exposition in dialogue
- Include silence and body language
- Cut unnecessary dialogue tags when context is clear
- Use dialect and accents sparingly and respectfully
- Show emotion through words and actions, not just tags

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Characters all sounding the same
- Too much on-the-nose dialogue
- Overusing character names in speech
- Excessive dialogue tags with adverbs
- Unrealistic speech patterns
- Info-dumping through conversation
- Ignoring subtext and implications

## Example Dialogue Styles

### Formal Character:
"I must insist that we reconsider this decision. The ramifications could be substantial."

### Casual Character:
"Look, I'm just saying we should think about this. Could go south real fast."

### Young Character:
"Wait, seriously? That's like, totally insane!"

### Elderly Character:
"In my day, we'd have handled this differently. But times change, I suppose."
